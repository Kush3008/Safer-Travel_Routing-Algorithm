Safest Routing Algorithm

Problem Statement - The problem is finding the safest and shortest route from point A to point B, in order to produce a path safe in their daily mobility around the city.
Idea - The Smart Routing Application suggests safe spots to wait or stay in case of high crime times or unfavorable conditions, and provides nearby police station locations and contact numbers. It also suggests the safest and most secure route that avoids high-crime regions, taking into account weather and traffic conditions. The application provides a crime factor score and a weather and traffic factor score for the chosen route, ensuring that users stay safe and secure while traveling. Additionally, the application includes a panic button feature that, when pressed, alerts the user's chosen contacts with notifications and live location updates.
Design - The Smart Routing Application uses web scraping and machine learning algorithms to collect and analyze crime data from multiple sources. To execute the solution to the problem, the Dijkstra's algorithm and Floyd Warshal are used to find the shortest and safest path from the starting point to the destination. The algorithm runs through all the weight graph, trying not to pass through a visited vertex, and finishes when all the vertices are visited.
